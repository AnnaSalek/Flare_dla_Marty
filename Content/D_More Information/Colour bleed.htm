Colour bleeding

Colour bleeding happens when one colour "bleeds" into the next once dry, i.e. it stains the area around. This is particulary the case with red or green icing laid on white background. It happens because your icing is too thin and/or has too much food coloring, among other reasons.

How to prevent color bleed?

  • Don't use too thin of a flood consistency
  • Use high quality food coloring
  • Allow the colors to develop (icing color gets darker over time, see section )
  • "Bleach" your white icing with white colouring.
  • Put your freshly decorated cookie to a dehydrator as fast as possible.
