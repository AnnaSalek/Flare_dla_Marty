Consistencies

One of the most unique elements of royal icing is that it can achieve a wide range of consistencies, i.e. different thicknesses. This is how you can achieve a totally thin/flat surface (with a flood consistency) or stiff flowers (piping consistency).

There are two main types of consistency:

Piping (thicker/holds its shape) and flood (thinner/can settle out to dry flat). Within these types, there is a wide range of consistencies (see the table below).

Consistency The look and feel Use Picture
Stiff The consistency you get right out of the mixer. 3D flowers (roses, leafs etc.) or similar objects and decorations that need to perfectly hold their shape.
Piping Resembles toothpaste being squeezed from a tube

For outlining, lettering, detailing, line work etc
Medium More liquid than piping consistency, but still holds its shape. For outlining and flooding with one consistency in order to create puffy areas (flower petals, for example).
Flood As the name suggests, it floods - like honey, for example.

For flooding the background or for wet-on-wet technique.

 

Is there a different recipe for each consistency?

The answer is NO! The way you make royal icing is to mix the icing to a stiff or medium peak (the thickest you’ll need) and then thin with liquid.

There is NO such thing as a perfect recipe for piping or flood consistency.
